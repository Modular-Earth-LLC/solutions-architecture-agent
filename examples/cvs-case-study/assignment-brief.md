## Candidate Instructions

As part of the interview process for the Principal Solution Architect role, candidates are expected to demonstrate their approach to the provided use cases through a structured and thoughtful methodology. Please choose one of the following two cases to complete and present on during your interview. The presentation will be about 15-20 minutes and then you will field questions from the panel. There will be opportunity for you to ask questions as well.

### Interview Expectations

1. **High-Level Solution Flow:** Candidates should present a high-level flow of their proposed solution at the outset. This should include an overview of the key components and how they interrelate within the context of the use case.
2. **Clarification and Questions:** Candidates are encouraged to ask questions or seek clarifications regarding the case study at the beginning of the interview. This will help ensure a clear understanding of the requirements and constraints before proceeding with the solution design.
3. **Diagram Development:** Candidates should be prepared to create and discuss diagrams during the interview. Visual representations of the solution are crucial for illustrating the architecture and flow of the proposed system.
4. **Multiple Solution Options:** Candidates should explore and present multiple options for their solution. For each option, they should discuss the pros and cons, considering factors such as feasibility, scalability, maintainability, and alignment with business objectives.
5. **Thought Process Articulation:** As the candidate walks through their diagrams, they should articulate their thought process clearly. This includes explaining the rationale behind design choices, integration strategies, and how they address the key considerations outlined in the use cases.
6. **Focus on Key Considerations:** Candidates should ensure that their solutions address the key considerations specified in the use cases, such as legacy system integration, IAM strategy, HCD principles, change management, and technology stack selection.

## Case Scenarios

> **Candidate selection (Paul):** Use Case 1 — Legacy System Transformation. Use Case 2 is retained below as context for understanding the organization's technology landscape, design preferences, and constraints.

### Use Case 1: Legacy System Transformation (SELECTED)

**Background:**
Our organization has a significant amount of legacy green screen applications running on IBMi (formerly known as AS/400). These applications are critical to our operations but present challenges in terms of user experience and accessibility. The business has identified the need for a comprehensive UI/UX transformation to modernize these applications, making them more user-friendly and visually appealing while ensuring they remain functional and efficient.

**Objective:**
We are looking for a Principal Solution Architect to lead this transformation initiative. The architect will be responsible for designing a new user interface and experience that meets the needs of our users while integrating with the existing legacy systems. Additionally, the architect will need to address Identity and Access Management (IAM) and Human-Centered Design (HCD) principles throughout the project.

**Key Considerations:**

1. **Legacy System Integration:** How will you ensure that the new UI/UX integrates seamlessly with the existing green screen applications? What strategies will you employ to minimize disruption to current operations?
2. **IAM Strategy:** What approach will you take to implement a robust Identity and Access Management system that secures user access to the new interface while maintaining compliance with industry standards?
3. **HCD Design Principles:** How will you incorporate Human-Centered Design principles into the development process? What methods will you use to gather user feedback and iterate on the design?
4. **Technology Stack:** What technology stack do you envision for this transformation? How will you balance modern technologies with the constraints of the legacy systems?
5. **Change Management:** Given that this transformation will significantly impact users accustomed to the legacy system, how will you manage the change process to ensure user adoption and minimize resistance?

### Use Case 2: Transitioning to a Domain-Based Architecture (NOT SELECTED — retained for organizational context)

**Background:**
Our organization currently relies on numerous legacy service layers that interface with monolithic legacy systems, including Mainframe and IBM i (IBMi). These services have become increasingly difficult to maintain and scale, leading to inefficiencies and redundancy. To address these challenges, the business has decided to transition to a domain-based architecture, creating a single source of truth based on distinct business domains.

**Objective:**
We are seeking a Principal Solution Architect to lead this transformation. The architect will be responsible for orchestrating the services in the new architecture while ensuring that integration patterns with legacy applications remain intact. The goal is to create a modern, cloud-based solution that can effectively manage data from legacy systems while reducing redundancy and improving overall efficiency.

**Key Considerations:**

1. **Domain Identification:** How will you identify and define the business domains that will serve as the foundation for the new architecture? What criteria will you use to ensure that these domains align with business objectives?
2. **Integration with Legacy Systems:** Given that some domains will be cloud-based while legacy applications continue to operate, what integration patterns will you implement to ensure seamless data flow between the cloud and legacy systems?
3. **Service Replacement Strategy:** Will you advocate for replacing legacy services with new services as-is, or will you take a more incremental approach? How will you determine which services to replace, refactor, or retire?
4. **Redundancy Reduction:** What strategies will you employ to identify and eliminate redundancy in the existing service layers? How will you ensure that the new architecture promotes reusability and efficiency?
5. **Common Capability Layer:** Can the experience layer call a common capability layer that depends on the domain layer? How will you design this architecture to ensure that it remains flexible and scalable, allowing each domain to choose its own cloud platform?
6. **Technology Stack:** What technology stack do you envision for this domain-based architecture? How will you ensure that it supports the integration of legacy systems while providing the necessary tools for modern development?

## Expectations and Tips

- A clear understanding of the challenges posed by legacy systems and how to address them.
- A comprehensive IAM strategy that includes user authentication, authorization, and role management.
- A commitment to HCD principles, including user research, prototyping, and usability testing.
- A well-defined technology stack that leverages modern frameworks while ensuring compatibility with legacy systems.
